# Introduction
### Communication in a connected world

Types of personal data in networking include direct identifiers like name, email, and phone number; online identifiers such as IP addresses and cookie IDs; and demographic or behavioral data like location, age, and browsing history. Other types include account and financial information, and biometric data. 

1. Identifiers

- Direct identifiers: Information that directly identifies an individual, such as a name, address, phone number, or ID number.

- Online identifiers: Data that identifies a device or user online, including IP addresses, MAC addresses, and cookie IDs. 

2. Personal characteristics

- Demographic data: Information about a person's characteristics, such as age, gender, and date of birth.

- Biometric data: Data that uniquely identifies a person, like fingerprints or facial patterns.
Health data: Information about an individual's health, which requires special protection. 

3. Behavioral and contextual data

- Location data: Information about a person's physical location, often collected via mobile devices.

- Browsing history: The websites a person visits and their online activity.

- Contact and account details: Information from user accounts, subscriptions, and other interactions.

- Financial information: Data like credit card numbers and bank account details.

- Declared interests: User-provided information such as likes, favorites, and preferences. 

### Terms
1. Volunteered data => Information that you intentionally give to a platform, app, or website. foreg: Filling out a signup form with your name, email, age or Posting a photo on Instagram

2. Inferred data => Information that the system predicts or calculates based on the volunteered + observed data. foreg: Netflix guessing what movies you like

3. Observed data => Information that a system collects by watching your behaviour or actions, without you typing it in. foreg: Your browsing history

4. Secret data => Sensitive information that is kept private and should not be publicly exposed because it can harm you if leaked. foreg: passwords, OTP codes, API keys
