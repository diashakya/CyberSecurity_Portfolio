Types of personal data in networking include direct identifiers like name, email, and phone number; online identifiers such as IP addresses and cookie IDs; and demographic or behavioral data like location, age, and browsing history. Other types include account and financial information, and biometric data. 

1. Identifiers

i. Direct identifiers: Information that directly identifies an individual, such as a name, address, phone number, or ID number.

ii. Online identifiers: Data that identifies a device or user online, including IP addresses, MAC addresses, and cookie IDs. 

2. Personal characteristics
i. Demographic data: Information about a person's characteristics, such as age, gender, and date of birth.

ii. Biometric data: Data that uniquely identifies a person, like fingerprints or facial patterns.
Health data: Information about an individual's health, which requires special protection. 

3. Behavioral and contextual data

i. Location data: Information about a person's physical location, often collected via mobile devices.

ii. Browsing history: The websites a person visits and their online activity.

iii. Contact and account details: Information from user accounts, subscriptions, and other interactions.

iv. Financial information: Data like credit card numbers and bank account details.

v. Declared interests: User-provided information such as likes, favorites, and preferences.
