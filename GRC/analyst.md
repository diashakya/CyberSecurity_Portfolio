- Governance = Set of rules, policies and operations that a company uses to achieve its business goals.

- Risk Management = Risk management helps businesses identify risks and find ways to remediate any that are found.

- Compliance = Compliance is the act of following rules, laws, and regulations.



## What a GRC analyst is ?
- GRC analysts are subject-matter experts on compliance and regulatory frameworks, and help ensure their employer or clients adhere to them.
- GRC analysts plays a pivotal role in getting an organization audit-ready, and are often found in fields with strong compliance requirements such as public sector organizations, healthcare, banking, software companies. 
- They support cybersecurity incident response by making sure incident response plans are up to date as well as other key tasks.

## GRC analyst's role and responsibilities

1. Acting as a subject-matter expert on relevant compliance and regulatory frameworks (E.g. HIPAA, ISO standards, PCI, SOC 2, GDPR, CCPA, etc), and staying on top of industry best practices.

2. Engaging in risk management and updating playbooks to align with current industry standards, regulatory changes, and best practices

3. Engaging in Disaster Recovery (DR) and Business Continuity Planning (BCP), managing the testing of these plans

4. Conducting compliance audits to ensure adherence to cybersecurity standards and regulations.

5. Monitoring compliance with regulations and standards, typically by key cybersecurity KPIs.

6. Engaging in Third-Party Risk Management (TPRM) by analyzing and minimizing risks associated with outstanding to third-party vendors or service providers.

7. Assisting with documentation following incident response.

8. Security awareness and training

9. Engaging in regulatory change management to make sure the companies policies and practices are adjusted following regulatory updates.

10. Preparing detailed reports and documentation of compliance findings and security gaps.

11. Developing and implementing controls to address cybersecurity and compliance needs across an organization.
